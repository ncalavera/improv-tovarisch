# Content Strategy Research & Hypotheses

## 1. Product Strategy & Personas

We have identified three primary personas "hiring" this documentation.

### Primary Persona
1.  **The Student (The "Growth" State)**
    *   **Context:** Post-workshop, commuting, deep study. Rehearsing 5-6 times before a show.
    *   **Goal:** Mastery, understanding "why" it works, fixing bad habits.
    *   **Needs:** Concepts, philosophy, video examples, "The Game" explained.

### Secondary Persona
2.  **The Coach / Leader**
    *   **Context:** Planning a rehearsal.
    *   **Goal:** Teaching materials, exercises to practice specific skills.
    *   **Needs:** Lesson plans, specific exercises for the format.

### Tertiary Persona
3.  **The Performer (The "Panic" State)**
    *   **Context:** Backstage, 15 mins before show.
    *   **Goal:** Quick recall of structure, timing, and "rules of the road."
    *   **Needs:** Visual anchors, bullet points, "Cheat Sheet."

## 2. Use Cases (Jobs to Be Done)

1.  **Rehearsal Prep (The "Briefing"):**
    *   Leader sends a link: "Read this before Tuesday."
    *   User needs to get the "gist" quickly so they aren't lost during practice.
2.  **Performance Support (The "Cheat Sheet"):**
    *   A physical or digital reference used right before the show.
    *   Needs to be printable or glanceable on a phone.
3.  **Deep Dive (The "Mastery"):**
    *   Long-form reading to internalize the art form.

## 3. Hypotheses to Validate

*   **H1 (The "Wall of Text"):** Performers ignore long descriptions when in "show mode." They need a visual map or timeline.
*   **H2 (The "Gap"):** Students know the *rules* (mechanics) but fail at the *game* (fun/strategy) because docs focus too much on structure and not enough on "why."
*   **H3 (The "Exercise" Need):** Knowing the format isn't enough; groups need specific exercises to practice the *skills* required for that format (e.g., "Group Mind" exercises for Harold).

---

## 4. Research Plan

**Goal:** Validate H1, H2, H3 and refine the content structure.
**Method:** Polls in community chats (Telegram/WhatsApp) and 1-on-1 interviews with improvisers.

### English Section (Internal Logic)

**Questions for Performers:**
1.  When you look up a format right before a show, what is the ONE thing you are usually looking for? (Timing? Opening rules? Edit techniques?)
2.  Do you prefer a written description or a visual diagram of the stage/structure?
3.  What is the most confusing format description you've ever read? Why?

**Questions for Coaches/Leaders:**
1.  What is the hardest part about teaching a new format?
2.  Do you create your own "cheat sheets" for your group? If yes, what's on them?
